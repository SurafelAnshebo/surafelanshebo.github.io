Selected Projects

1. Comparative Study of Vision Based Methods on UAV for Real-Time Traffic Monitoring
Presented at Xponential 2024 in San Diego
Read on ResearchGate(link)

This paper conducts a comparative analysis of traditional computer vision and deep learning-based vehicle detection and tracking algorithms using UAV for real-time traffic monitoring application. In conventional computer vision methods, the Haar cascade method is used with the Kanade-Lucas optical flow tracker. This technique is compared with deep learning-based methods YoloV7 with DeepSORT to detect and track vehicles. A qualitative and quantitative analysis is performed in simulation and then tested on an in-house built UAV. The goal is to support informed decision-making in perception and planning, ultimately enhancing safety in the industry. By evaluating these methods on a UAV platform, the research provides valuable insights for industry stakeholders to choose the most suitable approach for their specific requirements.

4. Image wrapping using 2D Homography
Github(link)

This project was part of my Computer Vision class assignment on fall 2023 taught by Dr Abbott. It is a project focused to employ mathematical principles and algorithms to calculate the homographic transformation, enabling the wrapping of one image onto another. In this project we have two images first is the rubicks cube and second one is the mandrill image. The mandrill image is wrapped onto the rubicks cube image.

5. Face detection for blinking an LED using serial communication
Github(link) YouTube(link)

This is a home project I did as an undergrad, the idea is I used my webcam to detect my face using Haar Cascade then if my face is detected LED hooked to my Arduino will blink. Whenever I detect a face, I will print, "Face is detected" then send serial command to my arduino which inturn turns my LED to ON. Conversely, if the face is not detected, I will print, "Face is not detected" then send serial command to my arduino which inturn turns my LED to OFF.

6. Boiler fuel tank level control system
YouTube(link)

This project was undertaken during my time in the Graduate Trainee Program at Coca-Cola Beverages Africa, working alongside my colleagues Robel and Rediet. At the time, the boiler system was experiencing redundant sensor failures, leading to costly and hazardous fuel overflows. Our approach began with thoroughly diagnosing the issue, followed by selecting an appropriate magnetic float level sensor. We then built a control board to manage the fuel levels—activating the pump when the level was low and stopping it when the level was high. Additionally, we incorporated a buzzer to alert operators if the fuel level became too high, enhancing safety and operational efficiency.
